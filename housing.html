Housing

History of housing and the class system

Previously, a person's social class had a big impact on the type of house they had. Those at the bottom of the social classes sometimes couldn’t afford a house and would live in a slum with lots of other people. They wouldn’t have beds or anywhere to wash as they couldn’t afford it or were unemployed and considered at the bottom of the class system.

At the opposite end of the scale, the upper classes would live in large houses with lots of spare bedrooms and have maids to clean and cook for them.

Modern day housing and the class system

Even today, a person’s class can have a big impact on the type of house they live in. There are still lots of homeless people at the bottom end of the class system, although there are some homeless shelters where they can stay a night and get some food.

Working class people can’t normally afford to rent expensive houses or to buy their own home, so they live in social housing, and even then they sometimes have to travel far from where they grew up or wanted to live to be able to get a house. The houses can also be very bad and have problems that make people ill, and in the worst cases, even die.

Middle class people can still struggle to buy their own home, but they can normally afford more expensive rent and live in nicer houses. They normally have a bedroom to themselves, unlike with social housing. If they are lucky they might have their own home, but it will still have a mortgage, so it’s not really theirs.

Upper class people can normally afford to have a nice house that doesn’t have a mortgage and that has extras that other people want, like garages and some even have swimming pools. These houses are large and can include mansions.
